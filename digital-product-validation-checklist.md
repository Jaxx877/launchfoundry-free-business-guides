# 21-Day Digital Product Validation Quick-Start Checklist

Use this checklist to test one digital-product idea, build the smallest useful version, and verify the complete customer journey before sending launch traffic.

## Days 1–7: investigate demand

- Day 1: define one recognizable buyer, painful moment, and current alternative.
- Day 2: review relevant requests, comments, reviews, and discussions for repeated language.
- Day 3: interview at least three qualified potential buyers about recent behavior.
- Day 4: write one bounded promise and a price hypothesis.
- Day 5: create a one-page description of the offer and next action.
- Day 6: show it to qualified prospects and record questions, objections, calls, or commitments.
- Day 7: decide whether to stop, narrow, retest, pilot, or build.

Compliments are encouragement. Stronger evidence includes a scheduled conversation, pilot commitment, preorder, deposit, or purchase from a qualified buyer.

## Days 8–14: build the smallest useful offer

- Map every decision and action between opening the product and producing the promised output.
- Remove bonuses and lessons that do not support the central workflow.
- Include one completed example.
- Provide editable worksheets, checklists, scripts, calculators, or templates.
- Follow the instructions from a clean folder and fix every confusing or missing step.

## Days 15–21: launch the complete journey

- Write a specific sales page covering the buyer, problem, result, contents, fit, exclusions, price, and next action.
- Prepare educational launch posts and emails without fake urgency.
- Package and test every delivered file.
- Verify the product, price, currency, checkout, success page, private download, and support path.
- Test the journey on desktop and mobile.
- Preview it with a small qualified group.
- Open the launch and record questions, checkouts, purchases, failures, and support issues.

## Seven evidence checks

Before expanding the product, verify:

1. The buyer is specific.
2. The problem is costly or repeated.
3. There is a reason to act.
4. The result is visible.
5. Qualified buyers are reachable.
6. The creator has relevant credibility.
7. The first promise is small enough to deliver well.
