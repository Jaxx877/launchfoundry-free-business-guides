# Digital Product Pricing Checklist

A first price is a testable business decision, not a statement about personal worth.

## Define the offer

- [ ] Name one buyer with a specific situation.
- [ ] Describe the costly problem in the buyer’s language.
- [ ] Define the smallest complete result the product supports.
- [ ] List exactly what is included and what is not.

## Compare real alternatives

- [ ] Doing nothing.
- [ ] Assembling free information.
- [ ] Hiring a service provider.
- [ ] Buying a competing product.
- [ ] Using an internal manual process.

## Check delivery economics

- [ ] Include platform, payment, support, refund, tax, and update costs.
- [ ] Estimate the time required to support one buyer.
- [ ] Set a price floor that does not make every sale a loss.
- [ ] Avoid claiming tax treatment or legal outcomes without professional advice.

## Run a small paid test

- [ ] Interview real buyers before expanding the product.
- [ ] Present one clear offer at one clear price.
- [ ] Measure purchases and qualified objections—not compliments.
- [ ] Change only one major variable at a time.
- [ ] Keep or change the price based on evidence gathered over a defined test period.
