# Freelancer Client Acquisition Quick-Start Checklist

Use this checklist to replace random applications and memory-based follow-up with a visible prospect-to-client workflow.

## 1. Write a narrow market statement

Complete this sentence:

> We help [specific buyer] achieve [visible result] when [costly situation] occurs, using [bounded service].

Treat the first version as a hypothesis. Improve it from real conversations rather than adding vague claims.

## 2. Choose two channels for eight weeks

- One **active-demand channel:** referrals, relevant requests, partner introductions, or a curated opportunity source.
- One **trust channel:** useful posts, case notes, workshops, niche communities, or practical articles.

Do not add more channels because one week feels quiet. Review qualified conversations after eight consistent weeks.

## 3. Research before outreach

Send a message only when you can identify:

- a truthful public observation;
- a plausible issue connected to your service;
- a small useful next step; and
- an easy, pressure-free way to decline.

A professional format is:

> I noticed [truthful observation]. If [related issue] is relevant this quarter, I can send a short outline of how I would approach it. If not, no reply needed.

## 4. Use discovery to decide

Assess the problem, urgency, impact, decision authority, access, constraints, alternatives, timeline, and working fit. End with a real decision: proposal, paid diagnostic, nurture date, referral, or closed as not a fit.

## 5. Protect both sides in the proposal

Include:

- context and objective;
- scope and deliverables;
- client responsibilities;
- exclusions and change control;
- timeline and milestones;
- price and payment terms; and
- the acceptance action that starts work.

## 6. Price from delivery economics

Account for direct delivery time, project management, revisions, administration, operating overhead, uncertainty, dependencies, and the target margin. When a budget is too small, change scope, timing, or support instead of pretending the original engagement is sustainable.

## 7. Run a weekly pipeline review

Update each opportunity, schedule one dated next action, close stale records, and review qualified conversations, proposals, wins, losses, cycle time, and source.
