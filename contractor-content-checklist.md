# 90-Day Contractor Content Quick-Start Checklist

Use this checklist to create consistent local-service content from real work without trying to become a full-time creator.

## 1. Establish four evidence pillars

- **Diagnose:** explain a question, symptom, inspection point, or decision that matters before work begins.
- **Explain:** translate one technical idea into plain language and clarify when a professional assessment may be appropriate.
- **Demonstrate:** show a real process detail, material choice, corrected problem, checklist, or completed detail.
- **Follow through:** explain estimates, preparation, scheduling, maintenance, warranties, and the next step after contact.

## 2. Use a realistic weekly rhythm

- Tuesday: answer one common customer question.
- Thursday: demonstrate one real process or project detail.
- Saturday: clarify preparation, maintenance, or the next action.
- Remaining days: respond to inquiries, capture future material, and improve useful posts.

## 3. Divide the 90 days into three phases

- Days 1–30 — **clarify:** services, boundaries, common questions, inspection points, and terminology.
- Days 31–60 — **prove:** real project lessons, processes, materials, corrected problems, and approved evidence.
- Days 61–90 — **follow through:** estimates, preparation, scheduling, maintenance, and next steps.

## 4. Protect customer trust

- Get permission before recording identifiable people, property, vehicles, documents, or conversations.
- Remove addresses, private paperwork, license plates, and personal information.
- State exactly what changed without implying one job represents every possible result.
- Keep education separate from diagnosis when the viewer has not received an inspection.
- Never invent, purchase, or materially rewrite a review.

## 5. Connect every post to follow-up

Every genuine inquiry needs:

- a contact method;
- the service location and need;
- a stage and responsible owner;
- the last contact date;
- one dated next action; and
- a final outcome and reason.

## 6. Measure useful evidence

Review qualified inquiries, estimate requests, meaningful replies, saves, response time, next-action completion, and jobs influenced by a known source. Popularity alone does not prove that the right customer understood the offer.
