# Completed Job-to-Content Checklist

Use one completed service job to create useful customer follow-up and trustworthy public education. Get permission before publishing identifiable people, property, addresses, messages, or results.

## Capture once

- [ ] Write the customer’s original problem in plain language.
- [ ] Record the constraint that made the job interesting: timing, access, diagnosis, materials, or coordination.
- [ ] Explain the decision made and why it fit the situation.
- [ ] Save one useful visual only after receiving permission.
- [ ] Record the verified result without exaggeration.
- [ ] Remove names, addresses, account details, and sensitive property information.

## Turn the record into useful outputs

- [ ] Send a customer follow-up with care or maintenance instructions.
- [ ] Draft one Google Business Profile update focused on the problem and lesson.
- [ ] Write one FAQ answering the question the customer was really asking.
- [ ] Create one short before/process/after explanation.
- [ ] Add the sanitized example to the appropriate service page.
- [ ] Ask for a review or referral only when the timing and customer experience justify it.

## Quality check

- [ ] Every claim can be supported by the job record.
- [ ] The content teaches something instead of merely announcing the job.
- [ ] Permission is documented.
- [ ] The call to action matches the content and is not pressured.
- [ ] Tracking identifies which page or channel produces a real inquiry.
