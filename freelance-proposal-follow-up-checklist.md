# Freelance Proposal Follow-Up Checklist

Follow up to make a decision easier—not to create pressure.

## Before sending

- [ ] Confirm the decision maker, scope, timing, and budget context.
- [ ] Put a clear next step and response date in the proposal.
- [ ] Record the opportunity stage and next action in one pipeline.

## Follow-up sequence

1. **Receipt:** Confirm the proposal arrived and ask whether anything is missing.
2. **Useful context:** Clarify one scope choice, risk, or implementation detail.
3. **Decision question:** Ask whether timing, budget, scope, or internal approval is the obstacle.
4. **Close the loop:** State that you will archive the active opportunity unless the buyer wants to continue.

## Stopping rule

- [ ] Stop repeated follow-up when the buyer will not provide a decision path.
- [ ] Move the lead to nurture instead of leaving it in the active pipeline.
- [ ] Keep notes factual and professional.
- [ ] Do not manufacture urgency or pretend availability is scarce.

## Measure

- [ ] Track proposal response rate.
- [ ] Track time from proposal to decision.
- [ ] Record the most common objection.
- [ ] Review which qualification gaps created stalled proposals.
